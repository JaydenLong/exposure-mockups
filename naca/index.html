TenTusscher et al. 2004: Na+/Ca+ Exchanger Current

The equation for this component in the Tusscher model is presented below.

          


The table below shows the parameters that make up the Na/Ca exchanger. Provided, is the 'trail' of papers that the value of the parameter is derived from as well as the link to these papers if they already exist in the physiome repository. The experimental species, tissue type and temperature are also given as these are useful to compare to the Tusscher model. The Tusscher model represents a human ventricular cell at 37 °C;.

The Na/Ca component and equation is taken from Noble et al. (1998) [7]. This formulation is improved from Luo & Rudy (1994) [1] by adding an extra parameter, alpha, that takes into account calcium in the diadic space. Luo & Rudy take this formulation from DiFrancesco & Noble (1985) [8] which in itself is a simplification of Mullins (1977) [9]. Noble et al., Luo & Rudy and Mullins all currently exist in the Physiome Repository whereas DiFrancesco and Noble does not. The related component names for these papers in the repository are sodium_calcium_exchanger, Na_Ca_exchanger and Na_Ca_exchanger respectively.
